Audio mastering is the final step in music production — the process of optimizing your finished mix for distribution. A professional master ensures your music sounds powerful, balanced and consistent on every playback system: earbuds, car speakers, club systems and all streaming platforms. Without mastering, your track will sound quiet or amateur compared to professionally released music.
Stem mastering involves sending your mix as grouped audio files — for example separate files for drums, bass, vocals and instruments. This gives us far greater control over the final sound than a stereo master allows. We can balance elements, control dynamics per group and achieve a significantly higher quality result. For artists who want the absolute best from their master — yes, it is absolutely worth it.
Send your mix as a 24-bit or 32-bit WAV or AIFF file at your session sample rate (44.1kHz, 48kHz or higher). Leave 3–6 dB of headroom — do not apply a limiter on the master bus. The higher the quality you send, the better the result we deliver. We accept files via WeTransfer, Dropbox and Google Drive.
